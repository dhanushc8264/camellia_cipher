Algorithm

Camellia is a symmetric block cipher that operates on 128-bit blocks and supports key sizes of 128, 192, or 256 bits. It uses a Feistel network with 18 rounds for 128-bit keys and 24 rounds for 192- and 256-bit keys. The algorithm includes functions like F-function, FL-function, and FL-1-function, which are crucial for encryption and decryption processes.
